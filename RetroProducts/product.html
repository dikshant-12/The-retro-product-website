The Amiga 500 Plus (often A500 Plus or simply A500+) is a revised version of
the original Amiga 500 computer. The A500+ featured minor changes to the
motherboard to make it cheaper to produce than the original A500. It was notable for introducing new versions of Kickstart and  Workbench.


The Amiga 600, also known as the A600, is a home computer introduced in March 1992. It is the final Amiga model based on the Motorola 68000 
and the 1990 Amiga Enhanced Chip Set. A redesign of the Amiga 500 Plus, it adds the option of an internal hard disk drive and a PCMCIA port.
